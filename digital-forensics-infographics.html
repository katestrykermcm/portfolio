Malware Infection & Deletion Timeline

An attacker infected Company ABC’s machines with malware. This attack timeline provides a comprehensive list of the targeted machines. Lines extend from the date that the malware was installed, to the date that it was eliminated. All malware lines end when the Incident Response teams took action in early November.

Phishing & Compromising Accounts

An attacker successfully gained access to Company ABC's employees’ OWA’s (Outlook Web Access) accounts, VPN’s, and direct deposit accounts. They realized they had been targeted only after the attacker wired money from these company accounts to his own account. To determine how exactly this huge loss transpired, Company ABC sought the expertise of the Digital Forensic team. This information graphic was used to summarize the examiners' findings by explaining the typical stages of a phishing attack and highlighting how deeply each of the involved employees entered into each stage. Employee O.G. was the first to be phished; once his account was compromised, the attacker was able to use his email to initiate a spear-phishing attack on a slew of O.G.'s coworkers.

Destroying Evidence of Illegal Activity

Company ABC accused two former employees, Lewis and Clark, of removing evidence of illegal activity from their machines. This infographic depicts the findings of digital forensic examiners confirming that a wiping tool was used to eliminate data on both machines, within a 12 hour period.

Reviewing Recording Practices

This graphic complemented a report chronicling Company ABC’s phone call recording practices over a 5 year period. It's purpose was to quickly convey the dates and locations lacking adequate coverage. Additionally, it reveals time periods when management was interested in reviewing their practices, by identifying dates when they accessed the back-ups. The infographic illustrates the number of calls recorded at each office location and the dates through which Company ABC was (i.) recording the phone calls as WAV files, (ii.) backing-up the WAV files to DVD, (iii.) accessing the primary WAV files, and (iv.) accessing the DVD backups.
