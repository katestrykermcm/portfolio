A Typical Project

For each project, I would meet with the specialized team of experts to be briefed on the case and their efforts. Once I had a deep understanding of the investigation and the story that the data illuminated, I would move forward with sketching and mocking up visualization options. The creation process was typically extremely iterative; I would constantly interface with the specialized experts to update and improve my work based on their findings, while getting their feedback to ensure clarity and accuracy.

Unfortunately for this section of my portfolio, nearly all of the work I completed at Stroz Friedberg for the Digital Forensics, Incident Response, and Security Science teams was strictly confidential. Therefore, I have almost no process work to share other than a few iterations of a recruiting project I completed for the Marketing team. This project adhered to the geometric Stroz Friedberg brand and the college campus recruiters' playful theme of a journey through their newest entry-level program. They were drawn to elements inspired by "The Game of Life" and early computer games.

Overview

To overly simplify, Stroz Friedberg helped their clients understand how attacks on their networks occurred (Digital Forensics), how to immediately respond as an attack was happening (Incident Response), and how to better protect themselves against future attacks (Security Science). I worked closely with experts on these different teams to understand the problems our clients faced. Then, I equipped the experts with intuitive, accurate, and concise representations to present their findings and recommendations to clients, who often did not have technical backgrounds. Although I completed over 50 information graphics and data visualizations over the course of my time at Stroz Friedberg, I only have access to this small subset of highly sanitized work because nearly every project was strictly confidential.
